\section*{Basics of amplitudes}

The 2-to-2 scattering of all massive ($m=1$) scalar particles is encoded by the S-matrix $S(s,t,u)$, satisfying elastic \textbf{unitarity} above threshold $s > 4$
$$
|S(s,t,u)|^2\leq1, 
\qquad
S(s,t,u)=1+i M(s,t,u)\,,
$$
with Mandelstam variables $s,t,u$. The amplitude $M$ satisfies \textbf{crossing} symmetry
$$
M(s,t)=M(t,s)=M(u,t), \qquad s+t+u=4
$$
as well as real \textbf{analyticity} 
$$
M(s^*,t^*)=M^*(s,t).
$$

\section*{Partial Wave decomposition}

Partial Wave expansion
$$
M(s,t)=\sum_{\ell=0}^{\ell_{\mathrm{max}}}n_{d,\ell}\,P_\ell(x)\,f_\ell(s)
$$
with
$
P_\ell(x)={}_2F_1\left(-\ell,d+\ell-3,\frac{d-2}{2},\frac{1-x}{2}\right)
$
and a conventional normalization factor $n_{d,\ell}$.
Inversing it using orthogonality of the $P_\ell(x)$ gives the partial wave coefficients
$$
f_\ell(s)=\int_{-1}^{1}\mathrm{d}x\,\Pi_\ell(x)\,M(s,t(s,x)), 
\qquad
x=\cos\theta=1+\frac{2t}{s-4}
$$
with kernel
$
\Pi_\ell(x)=\frac{\mathcal{N}_d}{2}\,(1-x^2)^{\frac{d-4}{2}}\,P_\ell(x), 
$
and $\mathcal{N}_d$ another conventional nromalization factor.
The unitarity condition becomes
$$
|S_\ell(s)|^2\leq 1\,,
\qquad
S_\ell(s)=1+\mathrm{i}\,\phi^2(s)\,f_\ell(s), 
$$
with $\phi(s)=(s-4)^{\frac{d-3}{4}}s^{-1/4}$.
Our normalization conventions read
$$
n_{d,\ell}(x)=\frac{(4\pi)^{d/2}(d+2\ell-3)\Gamma(d+\ell-3)}{\pi \Gamma\left(\frac{d-2}{2}\right)\Gamma(\ell+1)}\, \qquad
\mathcal{N}_d=\frac{(16\pi)^{\frac{d-2}{2}}}{\Gamma\left(\frac{d-2}{2}\right)}
$$
\section*{Wavelet ansatz}


We take the amplitude ansatz
$$
M(s,t)=\sum_{ijab\in \mathcal{D}_{ijab}}\alpha_{ijab}\,R_{\sigma_i\sigma_jab}(s,t)
$$
with the $\rho$-wavelet ansatz
$$
R_{\sigma_i\sigma_jab}(s,t)=\frac{n_d}{6}\,\left(\rho_{\sigma_i}^a(s)\rho_{\sigma_j}^b(t)+ s \leftrightarrow t \leftrightarrow u \right), 
$$
with $n_d=2^{5-d}n_{d,0}$ and the confomal map 
$$
\rho_\sigma(s)=\frac{\sqrt{\sigma-4}-\sqrt{4-s}}{\sqrt{\sigma-4}+\sqrt{4-s}}
$$
the complicated domain $\mathcal{D}_{ijab}$ stands for the wavelet centers indices $i,j\in \sigma_i=\{1,2,...,N_{\mathrm{max}}\}$ and $\rho$ powers $a,b\in \pi_i=\{0,1,...\}$ (we typically use max 1), together with the tricky condition to avoid double counting:
$
\sigma_i\leq\sigma_j \land 
a\leq b \land 
(a\neq0 \lor i\leq 1) \land 
(b\neq0 \lor j\leq 1) \land
a\leq \min(\pi_i,\pi_j) \land
b\leq \max(\pi_i,\pi_j)
$. 

\section*{Optimization problem}

The unitarity matrix is
$$
U_\ell(s)=\begin{pmatrix}
1-\frac{1}{2}\phi^2(s)\operatorname{Im} f_\ell(s) &
\phi(s)\operatorname{Re}f_\ell(s)  \\
\phi(s)\operatorname{Re}f_\ell(s) &
2\operatorname{Im}f_\ell(s)
\end{pmatrix}
$$
The low energy coefficients $c(n)$ are
$$
c(n)=\frac{\mathcal{N}_d}{2}\times 
\begin{cases}
c(0,0) & n=0 \\
\frac{1}{2}c(n,0) & n \text{ even} \\
c(n-1,1) & n \text{ odd}
\end{cases},
\qquad
c(n_1,n_2)=\frac{1}{n_1!n_2!}\partial_s^{n_1}\partial_t^{n_2}M(s,t)
$$
The game is then to optimize a given $c(n)$ using the semi-definite positive condition
$$
U_\ell(s)\succeq 0
$$
Sampled over $\ell=\{0,2,...,\ell_\mathrm{max}\}$ and $s=$ (some Chebychev grid with $s_{\mathrm{max}}$ typically 300 points $\in]4,\infty]$).

\section*{Improvements}

We may add Subtracted positivity constraints (SPC) 
$$
\operatorname{Im}\left(M(s,t)-
\sum_\ell^{\ell_\mathrm{max}} n_{d,\ell}
P_\ell\left(1+\frac{2t}{s-4}\right)
f_\ell(s)
\right)\geq0
$$
sampled over $s$ (same Chebychev grid as before) and also over $t$ for a few points (typically 10) very close to threshold $s=4^+$. These enters as linear constraints to the optimization problem. 

We also may add the Threshold (TH) improvement term
$$
M(s,t)\rightarrow M(s,t)+\alpha_{\mathrm{th}}\Delta(s,t)
$$
with
$\Delta(s,t)=\Delta(s)+s \leftrightarrow t \leftrightarrow u$, and 
$$
\Delta(s)=n_d\times\left(\frac{4-s}{4}\right)^{\frac{3-d}{2}}\times
\begin{cases}
i^d & d \text{ even} \\
\frac{i^{1+d}\pi}{\log{\frac{4-s}{4}}} & n \text{ odd} \\
\sin\left(\pi\frac{d-3}{2}\right) & \text{else}
\end{cases},
$$
which normalized so that $\alpha_{\mathrm{th}}\in[0,1]$.
